How This Heat Engine Works

This simulation demonstrates a Stirling-like heat engine operating through four thermodynamic processes:

  1. Isothermal Expansion: The gas expands at constant temperature (hot reservoir), doing work on the piston while absorbing heat.
  2. Isochoric Cooling: The gas cools at constant volume as heat flows to the cold reservoir.
  3. Isothermal Compression: The gas is compressed at constant temperature (cold reservoir), releasing heat.
  4. Isochoric Heating: The gas heats at constant volume as heat flows from the hot reservoir.

The efficiency is calculated as (Work Output) / (Heat Input). The Carnot efficiency represents the theoretical maximum efficiency for a heat engine operating between these temperatures.
