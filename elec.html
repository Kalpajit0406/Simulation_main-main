About Electron Movement in Metals

In metallic bonding, atoms release some of their electrons to be shared among all atoms in the structure. These "delocalized" electrons move freely throughout the metal lattice.

This simulation shows:

- Metal atoms (gray spheres) in a crystal lattice

- Delocalized electrons (blue spheres) moving freely

- Electron paths showing their random movement
